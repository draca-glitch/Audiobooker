The bench at the edge of the park had a view of the lake, and Anna had been sitting on it for almost an hour before she noticed the man with the dog.

He looked up from his book and nodded politely. The dog, a small grey terrier, sniffed at her boots.

"He won't bite," Marcus said. "He just likes new shoes."

"That's lucky," Anna replied, scratching the terrier behind one ear. "Mine are practically new."

Marcus closed his book and tucked it under his arm. "Are you reading anything good lately?"

"Half a dozen things. None of them well." She laughed. "I keep starting novels and abandoning them at the third chapter."

"That's the most honest description of reading I've ever heard."

The terrier had given up on the boots and was now investigating a fallen leaf with great seriousness. Anna watched him for a moment.

"Does he have a name?"

"Pip. My daughter named him when she was nine. She was going through a phase."

"Phases are how people end up with terriers."

"Exactly." Marcus glanced at the lake. The wind had picked up and the surface was beginning to ripple. "I should probably head back before it rains."

"It looks like it might."

He stood, whistled softly, and Pip trotted obediently to his side. "Nice meeting you, Anna."

She smiled. "How do you know my name?"

Marcus pointed at the small leather tag on her bag, where her name was stitched in faded thread.

"Ah." She laughed. "Of course."

He raised a hand in farewell and walked off down the path, the terrier trotting beside him, both of them disappearing slowly into the grey afternoon.
