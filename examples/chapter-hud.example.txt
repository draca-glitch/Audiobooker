The corridor lights flickered once and then steadied into a soft amber. Vance stopped where he was and looked up at the ceiling, listening.

"Tell me you saw that," he said.

[Local power fluctuation: 3 percent under nominal.]
[Cause: unknown. Filtering subsystems holding.]

Rell appeared from the side passage, tablet in hand, her face washed pale by its glow. "Saw it. Logged it. Whatever's down there isn't running on the same grid we are."

"Comforting."

"It wasn't meant to be."

[Air composition: trace ozone, plus zero point four ppm.]
[Recommendation: keep moving.]

Vance let out a slow breath and checked the strap on his pack out of habit. He hadn't been this far into the lower decks in eleven months and he was very aware of it.

<!-- voice: Aurora -->
*You are not alone in this passage. I am here. Continue forward when you are ready.*

The voice came from nowhere and everywhere at once, the way Aurora's voice always did. Rell glanced at him, eyebrow raised.

"Did she just—"

"Yeah. She does that now." Vance started walking again. "She says it makes her feel less abstract."

"And you let her?"

"I couldn't stop her if I wanted to."

[Forward sensor array: clear to next junction.]
[Notice: ambient temperature dropping one point two degrees per minute.]

Rell zipped her jacket up to her throat. "I really hope this is just a maintenance fault."

<!-- voice: Aurora -->
*It is not. But you will be fine.*

"You don't actually know that," Rell said, more to the air than to anyone in particular.

A long pause. Then:

<!-- voice: Aurora -->
*No. But I prefer to say it.*

Vance almost smiled. They walked on, the amber lights flickering once more behind them, and the corridor grew imperceptibly colder with every step.
